About Ashurah

Ashurah is observed on the 10th day of Muharram, the first month of the Islamic calendar. It is a day of reflection, gratitude, fasting, and remembrance of important events in Islamic history.

The Importance of Ashurah

Ashurah holds great significance in Islam. The Prophet ﷺ encouraged fasting on this day as an act of worship and gratitude to Allah.

It reminds Muslims of patience, faith, sacrifice, and trust in Allah during difficult times.

Significance of Ashurah

Fasting

Fasting on Ashurah is a Sunnah and a means of seeking Allah's mercy.

Patience

A reminder to remain patient and trust Allah in every situation.

Gratitude

A day to thank Allah for His countless blessings and guidance.

Faith

Strengthens faith through reflection and remembrance.
